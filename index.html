How Likely are Different Qualitative Outcomes for Foundation Model Agents?

Introduction

We believe that AGI could soon be achieved with foundation model agents (FMAs): systems like AutoGPT and Devin that equip foundation models with memory, tool use, and other affordances so they can perform multi-step tasks autonomously. We should carefully analyze how classic AGI risk arguments might play out if we get AGI with FMAs.

In this post, we try to systematically analyze how each behavior-shaping mechanism could make things go well or poorly. More specifically, we enumerate behavior-shaping mechanisms and qualitative outcomes (i.e. ways things could go well or poorly), then execute the following nested for loop.

For each behavior-shaping mechanism B:
        For each qualitative outcome Q:
                How can B make Q more or less likely if we get AGI with FMAs?
                How strong are these effects?

The behavior-shaping mechanisms we consider are:

  1. Pretraining
  2. Finetuning
  3. Whitebox interventions
  4. Scaffolding
  5. Inputs
  6. Learning / reasoning at runtime

The qualitative outcomes we consider are:

  1. Malicious use
  2. Natural adversarial inputs
  3. Scheming / rogue AI
  4. Hidden serial reasoning
  5. You get what you measure
  6. Multi-agent conflict
  7. Successful alignment with a “Do What I Mean and Check” (DWIMAC) alignment target
